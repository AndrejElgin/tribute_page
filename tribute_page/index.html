Albert Camus

Albert Camus was born in Algeria at 1913. He always wanted to be an author and despite he lived in poverty all of his childhood he managed to study in university of Algeria. Young Albert was attracted by sport but tuberculosis cut this plans short. Because of tuberculosis Camus decided to live near the Alps in a secluded area. He wrote a couple of theatre plays, although he is not famous for them. One of the most significant contributions that he made was his role as an editor of newspaper Combat. It was a resistence newspaper in France occupied by Nazi Germany. In 1942 his first novel(the Outsider) was published. It was well percieved by public at the time. He also wrote a couple of philosophical essays. In them he tried to refute nihilism.

His next major succes was the novel called The Plague>. Which was written in 1947. In can be percieved as his philosophical essays transformed into a fiction book. This novel describes attemts of citizens of small city to oppose the epidemic. in 1957 Albert Camus recieved a Noble Prize.
